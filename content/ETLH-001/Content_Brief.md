# ETLH-001 Content Brief

## Status

Approved v1.0

## Topic

Decision fatigue / unnecessary friction

## Working Title

Why Life Feels Harder Than It Needs To Sometimes

## Published Article Title

Why Life Feels Harder Than It Needs To Sometimes

## Slug

why-life-feels-harder-than-it-needs-to-sometimes

## Core Reader

A renter or small-space dweller who feels mentally drained by ordinary daily life and wants practical, non-overwhelming ways to make life feel lighter.

## Reader State

Overwhelmed, tired, possibly self-critical, but open to one small practical improvement.

## Core Problem

The reader may be spending attention, time, and mental energy on repeated small decisions, clutter, unfinished tasks, and systems that create unnecessary friction.

## Primary Realization

Maybe life is not harder because I am failing. Maybe I am carrying unnecessary friction that can be reduced.

## Core ETLH Principle

Reduce unnecessary friction so thoughtful living becomes easier.

## Supporting Research Summary

Research on decision fatigue, cognitive load, choice overload, and values-based behavior suggests that people benefit from reducing unnecessary cognitive demands, simplifying choice environments, creating supportive systems, and translating meaningful intentions into small repeatable actions.

## Key Metaphor

A backpack slowly filling with small stones. One stone is manageable; fifty become heavy.

## Tone

Calm, practical, encouraging, non-judgmental, evidence-informed, never guilt-based.

## Must Include

- Relatable ordinary-life opening
- Explanation of unnecessary friction
- Distinction between meaningful effort and unnecessary burden
- One clear Eco Step
- Reflection question
- Evidence confidence note

## Must Avoid

- Overclaiming science
- Saying willpower simply runs out like a battery
- Shame, guilt, hustle language, or productivity bro tone
- Too many action steps

## Eco Step

Before tomorrow begins, remove one unnecessary source of friction, such as giving keys a permanent home, planning lunch, writing down something you are trying to remember, unsubscribing from emails, or placing one frequently used item where it naturally belongs.

## Reflection Question

What can I make easier so I have more energy for what truly matters?
