# ETLH-001 Instagram Carousel

## Status

Visuals approved — pending export/posting

## Source

Based on content/ETLH-001/Content_Brief.md and content/ETLH-001/Master_Article.md

## Format

Instagram carousel, 8 slides, 4:5 vertical format recommended.

## Carousel Objective

Help the reader recognize unnecessary friction in ordinary daily life and choose one small action that makes tomorrow feel lighter.

## Slide 1

Headline:
Why does everyday life feel so exhausting sometimes?

Body:
It might not be because you're failing.
It might be because too many small things are asking for your attention.

Visual Direction:
Calm domestic scene. A person standing near a kitchen counter, keys, mail, phone, and small clutter visible. Gentle natural light.

## Slide 2

Headline:
Your brain has been working all day.

Body:
Every decision.
Every reminder.
Every interruption.
Every unfinished task.

They all take a little energy.

Visual Direction:
Minimal illustration or lifestyle image showing scattered mental inputs: phone, notes, keys, open fridge, mail.

## Slide 3

Headline:
This is unnecessary friction.

Body:
Small sources of effort that drain energy without giving much value back.

Visual Direction:
Backpack metaphor with small stones, or a person carrying several small everyday items.

## Slide 4

Headline:
Not all effort is bad.

Body:
Caring for people.
Learning.
Healing.
Keeping promises.

Some effort matters.

Visual Direction:
Warm, meaningful human moment. Hands making tea, caring gesture, studying, or quiet focused work.

## Slide 5

Headline:
The goal is not a perfect life.

Body:
The goal is to stop carrying weight that never needed to be there.

Visual Direction:
Person setting down a bag, clearing a small surface, or removing one item from a cluttered space.

## Slide 6

Headline:
Ask one better question.

Body:
What keeps asking for my attention without giving anything meaningful back?

Visual Direction:
Notebook with a simple question written on the page. Calm desk setup.

## Slide 7

Headline:
One small change is enough.

Body:
Give your keys a home.
Write down one reminder.
Plan tomorrow's lunch.
Unsubscribe from one email.
Move one item where it belongs.

Visual Direction:
Simple before/after moment. Keys in a bowl, lunch packed, notebook reminder, clean counter.

## Slide 8

Headline:
Your Eco Step

Body:
Before tomorrow begins, remove one unnecessary source of friction.

Make tomorrow a little lighter than today.

Footer CTA:
Save this for your next reset.

Visual Direction:
Peaceful evening or morning scene. Calm home, soft light, sense of relief.

## Caption

Sometimes life feels heavier than it should because too many small things are quietly asking for your attention.

The goal is not to optimize your whole life.

Start smaller.

Notice one source of unnecessary friction.
Remove one small burden.
Make tomorrow a little lighter than today.

Eco Step:
Before tomorrow begins, choose one thing that repeatedly drains your attention and make it easier.

Save this for your next reset.
