## Subject

When the food-scrap bin already smells

## Preview text

Reset the pail, check the destination, and change one part of the removal cadence.

## Body

A food-scrap routine can fail for very ordinary reasons: residue under a lid, scraps sitting warm longer than expected, a liner the local program does not accept, or a drop-off cadence that does not fit the week.

The new ETLH guide starts with the bin that already smells. It walks through a detergent-and-water reset, fruit-fly source checks, counter versus cold staging, and the difference between collection, worm composting, and bokashi fermentation. It also includes a full hand-washing path—no dishwasher assumed.

No method is presented as odor-free or pest-proof, and local acceptance rules remain local.
