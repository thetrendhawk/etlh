# Glossary

## Purpose

Define recurring ETLH language so brand, editorial, research, design, and publishing work uses terms consistently.

## Scope

This glossary covers established ETLH concepts and repository production terms. Definitions marked as reconciled were reconstructed from surviving approved evidence rather than copied from a recoverable original guide.

## Status

Active v1.0.

## Core ETLH Terms

### Eco Step

A small, concrete, repeatable action that makes life lighter, calmer, more sustainable, or more aligned.

An Eco Step should fit the reader's real constraints and be useful enough to repeat without requiring a complete lifestyle overhaul.

### Friction

Anything that consumes attention, energy, or time without meaningfully supporting the person's values, goals, or well-being.

### Unnecessary Friction

Avoidable resistance created by repeated searches, excess decisions, poor placement, visual clutter, unnecessary steps, unfinished systems, or demands that give little back.

### Meaningful Effort

The useful effort that remains after unnecessary friction is removed. Meaningful effort supports the person's values, goals, relationships, home, health, or well-being.

### Friction Finder Question

> What keeps asking for my attention without giving much back?

A repeatable ETLH diagnostic question used to notice recurring sources of unnecessary friction without blame or overwhelm.

### Focused Consistency

Small, measurable actions taken repeatedly in a coherent direction.

ETLH principle:

> Focused consistency creates lasting change.

### Diffused Attention

Attention or effort scattered across too many directions, making meaningful progress harder to sustain.

### Progress Over Perfection

The principle that useful forward movement matters more than flawless execution or idealized sustainable living.

ETLH wording:

> Progress matters more than perfection.

### Thoughtful Living

Directing attention, effort, money, and daily choices toward what meaningfully supports a person's values, goals, home, and well-being.

### Trust as Currency

The principle that ETLH's long-term credibility and reader relationship are more valuable than short-term attention, promotion, or monetization.

ETLH wording:

> Trust is the main currency.

### Stepping-Stone Framework

The reconciled ETLH change sequence:

> Notice → Choose → Reduce → Repeat → Align

Each step narrows a broad intention into one practical improvement that can be repeated and connected to what matters.

### Notice

Identify a recurring source of friction, often by using the Friction Finder Question.

### Choose

Select one friction point rather than attempting to change everything at once.

### Reduce

Remove one unnecessary step, obstacle, decision, or demand.

### Repeat

Turn the improvement into an Eco Step or supportive system that can be used again.

### Align

Direct recovered attention, time, money, or energy toward values, calm, sustainability, savings, health, relationships, or purpose.

### One-Home Rule

If an everyday item is repeatedly lost, moved, or searched for, give it one obvious and easy-to-use home.

### Doorway Reset

A simple entryway system that helps a person leave more easily, return more calmly, and stop losing recurring items such as keys, bags, mail, and shoes.

### Sunday Surface Reset

A low-pressure weekly reset in which one frequently seen surface is cleared by removing what does not belong, grouping what stays, and leaving one open space.

### Habit Friction

Repeated obstacles that make a useful or sustainable choice harder to perform. Habit friction can often be reduced through visibility, readiness, proximity, fewer decisions, and simpler placement.

## Production Terms

### Content Package

The complete set of source, strategy, copy, visuals, accessibility material, publishing notes, and revision history created for one ETLH topic or channel asset.

### Cornerstone Article

A substantial, authoritative ETLH article that explains a central topic and supports derivative content across multiple channels.

### Research Dossier

A structured evidence record containing source material, verbatim quotes where available, categories, emotions, jobs-to-be-done, constraints, existing solutions, evidence strength, exclusions, and synthesis notes.

### Master Content Brief

The approved strategic brief that defines the reader, problem, realization, evidence, tone, required elements, prohibited claims, and derivative assets for a topic.
