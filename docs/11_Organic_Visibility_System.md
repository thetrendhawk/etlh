# ETLH Organic Visibility System

## Purpose

This document defines how Eco Tiny Living Hub plans, researches, creates, packages, publishes, connects, refreshes, and measures organic content.

It is an operating system, not a claim that any keyword, topic, channel, or article will perform. Search demand, ranking difficulty, audience response, and conversion behavior must be established with evidence before they are treated as facts.

## Operating principles

1. **Reader problems come before keywords.** Begin with a specific problem, constraint, question, or desired outcome experienced by people living sustainably in small spaces.
2. **Evidence comes before claims.** Do not invent demand, outcomes, savings, credentials, product performance, or reader experiences.
3. **One useful source, multiple channel packages.** The article is the durable source; Pinterest, Instagram, and email/download assets are distinct packages adapted from it rather than duplicated verbatim.
4. **Trust outranks traffic.** Avoid clickbait, unsupported urgency, thin affiliate content, and recommendations that do not leave the reader better informed.
5. **Focused consistency creates lasting change.** Build coherent topic coverage and improve it over time instead of scattering effort across unrelated trends.
6. **Measurement informs decisions; it does not manufacture certainty.** Record what happened, distinguish observation from interpretation, and avoid drawing conclusions from insufficient data.

## Workflow overview

Each organic content initiative moves through these stages:

1. Problem and audience evidence
2. Search and content opportunity assessment
3. Topic-cluster mapping
4. Search-content brief
5. Article production and editorial review
6. Metadata, schema, image, and internal-link review
7. Publication and technical validation
8. Pinterest, Instagram, and email/download packaging
9. Indexing and distribution checks
10. Performance review and content refresh

A piece may pause at any stage when evidence is weak, the scope is unclear, or a required owner decision is missing.

## 1. Problem and audience evidence

### Required inputs

A proposed topic should be supported by one or more traceable sources, such as:

- first-hand public discussions;
- search-query data from an owned analytics or search platform;
- comments or questions received by ETLH;
- product reviews describing practical constraints;
- reputable studies, public agencies, standards bodies, or subject-matter sources;
- ETLH's approved market-research records.

### Evidence record

For each problem, record:

- source and retrieval date;
- exact wording or a faithful paraphrase;
- audience and living context;
- problem category;
- emotional context;
- job to be done;
- constraints;
- existing attempted solutions;
- evidence strength;
- exclusion flags for promotional, generic, duplicate, or unsupported material.

Do not convert an isolated anecdote into a broad market claim.

## 2. Search and content opportunity assessment

Research should separate observed facts from judgment.

### Evidence fields

Record, when available:

- query or problem wording;
- source/tool and retrieval date;
- geography and language;
- estimated demand metric and its source;
- trend direction and period;
- current result types;
- dominant search intent;
- notable competing pages;
- evidence gaps;
- confidence level.

### Decision fields

Then document the editorial judgment separately:

- ETLH audience fit;
- trust and usefulness potential;
- ability to offer original value;
- evidence burden;
- production effort;
- monetization relevance, if any;
- priority recommendation.

Third-party keyword scores are estimates, not ground truth. Do not report them as verified traffic or guaranteed opportunity.

## 3. Topic-cluster model

A cluster contains one broad cornerstone resource and a set of narrower supporting articles that answer distinct questions or constraints.

### Cluster requirements

A cluster should have:

- one clearly defined reader outcome;
- a bounded audience and context;
- a cornerstone page broad enough to orient the reader;
- supporting articles with non-duplicative intent;
- natural internal links between related pages;
- enough evidence to justify production;
- a refresh owner or review cadence.

### Candidate status

The roadmap currently lists these as **initial candidates only**:

- sustainable small-space living;
- low-waste small kitchens;
- calm and organization in small spaces;
- sustainable living on a budget.

They are not approved priority clusters until research records support the selection. Selecting the first three clusters must document why they were chosen and why alternatives were deferred.

## 4. Search-content brief

Create one brief before drafting each search-led article.

### Brief fields

- working title;
- target reader and living context;
- primary problem;
- desired reader outcome;
- primary search intent;
- primary query wording, if evidence supports one;
- related questions and subtopics;
- scope boundaries;
- required evidence and source types;
- claims requiring verification;
- prohibited or unsupported claims;
- proposed outline;
- original-value requirement;
- internal links to add;
- relevant existing pages to avoid duplicating;
- recommended visuals and accessibility requirements;
- metadata notes;
- structured-data eligibility;
- Pinterest packaging angle;
- Instagram packaging angle;
- email/download relationship;
- conversion or next-step purpose;
- review and refresh date.

A brief must not include invented search volume, rankings, product outcomes, testimonials, or credentials.

## 5. Article production standards

Each article should:

- answer the reader's primary question early;
- distinguish evidence, practical guidance, estimates, and opinion;
- state meaningful limitations and tradeoffs;
- use descriptive headings and accessible language;
- avoid unnecessary repetition written only to target keywords;
- link to primary or authoritative sources for material factual claims;
- disclose affiliate relationships where applicable;
- avoid presenting an affiliate product as necessary when a lower-cost or non-purchase option exists;
- comply with the Editorial Manual, Research Manual, publishing validation, and legal pages already in the repository.

## 6. Internal-linking rules

Internal links should help readers continue a task, understand a prerequisite, compare alternatives, or move between cornerstone and supporting pages.

### Required practices

- Link supporting articles to the relevant cornerstone page when useful.
- Link cornerstone pages to supporting articles in context, not only in a generic list.
- Use descriptive anchor text.
- Avoid multiple competing pages targeting the same intent without a deliberate differentiation plan.
- Review existing relevant pages before publishing a new one.
- Add reciprocal links only where they are genuinely useful.
- Do not create orphan articles.

Internal links are editorial navigation, not a quota. Do not insert irrelevant links solely to increase link counts.

## 7. Metadata, schema, and image requirements

Before publication, verify:

- unique title and meta description;
- canonical URL on the preferred production host;
- Open Graph and social metadata;
- descriptive image alt text;
- explicit image dimensions and repository image-budget compliance;
- article dates supported by the source record;
- article structured data only when the page qualifies;
- no fabricated author credentials, ratings, reviews, FAQ content, or organization facts;
- sitemap and route inclusion;
- valid internal links and no placeholders.

Organization or WebSite structured data requires a separate technical review and must use only owner-verified facts.

## 8. Channel packaging separation

### Article

The article is the durable, searchable source. It carries the complete evidence, nuance, limitations, links, and update history.

### Pinterest

Pinterest packaging may include:

- a search-aligned pin title;
- a concise description;
- a visual concept;
- accessible alt text;
- a direct link to the relevant article or resource.

Do not make the pin promise more than the destination delivers.

### Instagram

Instagram packaging may include:

- carousel or Reel angle;
- concise reader-centered copy;
- caption;
- hashtags;
- first comment;
- alt text.

Instagram should adapt one useful idea to the platform rather than compressing the whole article into promotional copy.

### Email or download

Email/download packaging should provide a logical next step: a checklist, worksheet, summary, sequence, or practical implementation aid. It must not manufacture scarcity or imply results that ETLH cannot substantiate.

## 9. Publication and distribution checks

After publication:

- confirm the production page returns the expected status;
- verify canonical, metadata, structured data, images, links, and responsive behavior;
- confirm sitemap inclusion;
- record the deployment and validation evidence;
- request indexing or inspect coverage through an owner-authorized search platform when appropriate;
- publish only the channel packages that have been reviewed and point to the correct destination;
- record publication dates and URLs in the content system.

## 10. KPI baseline and interpretation

Track only metrics available from verified owned sources. Potential fields include:

- indexed pages;
- search impressions;
- clicks;
- click-through rate;
- ranking bands rather than false precision;
- organic landing pages;
- engaged sessions or equivalent approved analytics measure;
- email/download conversions;
- affiliate or other conversions where tracking and disclosure are valid;
- Pinterest referral traffic;
- Instagram referral traffic;
- content age and refresh status.

### Measurement rules

- Store source, property, date range, filters, and retrieval date.
- Preserve raw exports where practical.
- Compare like with like.
- Note tracking changes, consent limitations, seasonality, low sample sizes, and publication age.
- Do not attribute causation from correlation alone.
- Do not create historical baselines from memory.
- Mark unavailable metrics as unavailable rather than estimating them.

The durable KPI baseline and external uptime monitoring remain separate roadmap tasks.

## 11. Content-refresh rules

Review a page when one or more of these conditions applies:

- material facts, prices, regulations, products, or external sources may have changed;
- the article has broken or redirected links;
- Search Console shows sustained impressions with weak click-through rate;
- the page loses meaningful visibility over a sufficiently representative period;
- reader questions reveal a missing explanation;
- a new supporting article changes the best internal-link structure;
- the article no longer meets current evidence, accessibility, image, metadata, or disclosure standards.

A refresh record should state:

- why the review occurred;
- evidence inspected;
- changes made;
- claims reverified;
- links added or removed;
- publication/update date decision;
- validation results.

Do not change a displayed article date for cosmetic freshness alone.

## 12. Roles and approval boundaries

### Safe operational work

The content system may autonomously:

- organize verified research;
- draft briefs from documented evidence;
- identify duplication and internal-link gaps;
- prepare factual content and channel packages;
- run repository validation;
- document measurements from authorized sources.

### Owner or specialist decisions

Pause when work requires:

- legal interpretation or policy commitments;
- private credentials or account access not already authorized;
- paid tools, purchases, or subscriptions;
- destructive account or property changes;
- owner-only verification of identity, credentials, business facts, or financial results;
- approval of sensitive claims or regulated guidance.

## 13. Definition of done

An organic content initiative is complete only when:

- the underlying problem and opportunity evidence are recorded;
- the brief is approved under the applicable workflow;
- the article meets editorial and research standards;
- metadata, schema eligibility, images, internal links, and disclosures are verified;
- repository CI and deployment gates pass;
- production is validated;
- channel packages point to the correct published destination;
- measurement and refresh records are initialized without invented data.
