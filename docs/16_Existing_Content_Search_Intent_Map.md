# ETLH Existing Content Search-Intent Map

## Purpose

This document maps the current ETLH article inventory to its most defensible search intent, cluster role, overlap risk, and next editorial action.

This is an editorial classification of existing content. It does **not** claim verified search volume, ranking potential, traffic, or competitive difficulty. Those require the evidence process in `12_Search_Research_Methodology.md`.

## Intent labels

- **Informational — how-to:** the reader wants steps or a process.
- **Informational — ideas:** the reader wants examples, options, or inspiration.
- **Informational — checklist:** the reader wants a bounded action list.
- **Informational — problem/solution:** the reader wants help understanding and reducing a recurring problem.
- **Commercial investigation:** the reader is comparing products or purchase paths. No current article is primarily assigned this intent.

## Cannibalization findings

### Budget and beginner habit overlap

Three articles currently compete for nearly the same broad reader need:

1. `15 Low Waste Lifestyle Tips You Can Start This Week`
2. `21 Easy Sustainable Habits on a Budget (Perfect for Renters)`
3. `Sustainable Living in an Apartment: 10 Easy Habits`

Recommended structure:

- **Cornerstone:** `21 Easy Sustainable Habits on a Budget (Perfect for Renters)`
- **Narrow support:** `15 Low Waste Lifestyle Tips...` focused only on waste reduction
- **Reposition or consolidate:** `Sustainable Living in an Apartment: 10 Easy Habits` focused on renter-controlled apartment utilities and constraints, or merged after performance evidence exists

### Decor overlap

The living-room guide and apartment before/after article can coexist if their jobs remain distinct:

- Living-room guide = durable layout and styling hub
- Before/after article = documented transformation proof

Without real visual evidence, the second article does not currently fulfill its distinct role.

### Kitchen overlap

The three kitchen articles form a coherent preliminary cluster:

- Pantry organization = visibility, rotation, and storage system
- Chef-inspired habits = food-waste prevention and ingredient use
- Budget swaps = recurring-use and packaging substitutions

They should cross-link intentionally and avoid repeating generic reusable-bottle, cloth, and compost advice in every article.

## Preliminary cluster signals from the existing inventory

The inventory already has the strongest depth in these three areas:

1. **Small-space zero-waste kitchen** — three differentiated articles, with clear opportunities around food storage, composting, meal planning, leftovers, and renter constraints.
2. **Budget sustainable habits for renters** — multiple articles but substantial duplication that must be resolved before expansion.
3. **Sustainable small-space decor** — three articles with good reader fit, but the visual-proof standard is currently the largest weakness.

These are inventory-strength observations, not final priority-cluster approval. Final cluster selection must combine audience evidence, search evidence, brand fit, execution feasibility, and cannibalization review.

## Immediate editorial priorities

1. Repair or reframe both articles using “Before & After.”
2. Establish the 21-habits article as the broad renter-habits hub and differentiate or consolidate the overlapping lists.
3. Add intentional cross-links among the three kitchen articles.
4. Replace unsupported certainty in savings, environmental-impact, air-quality, and authority-based claims.
5. Add visual requirements to every transformation, layout, pantry, and decor brief before publication.
