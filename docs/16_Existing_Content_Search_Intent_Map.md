# ETLH Existing Content Search-Intent Map

## Purpose

Map all 14 current ETLH articles to their most defensible search intent, cluster role, overlap risk, claims or proof risk, and next editorial action.

This is an editorial classification of repository content. It does **not** claim verified search volume, ranking potential, traffic, conversion performance, or competitive difficulty. Those require the evidence process in `12_Search_Research_Methodology.md`.

## Intent labels

- **Informational — how-to:** the reader wants steps or a process.
- **Informational — ideas:** the reader wants examples, options, or inspiration.
- **Informational — checklist:** the reader wants a bounded action list.
- **Informational — problem/solution:** the reader wants help understanding and reducing a recurring problem.
- **Informational — transformation:** the reader expects a documented starting state, actions, and result.
- **Commercial investigation:** the reader is comparing products or purchase paths. No current article is primarily assigned this intent.

## Cannibalization findings

### Budget renter-habits overlap

Five articles compete across beginner, renter, budget, checklist, low-waste, and easy-habit intent:

1. `easy-sustainable-habits-on-a-budget`
2. `low-waste-lifestyle-tips-beginners`
3. `sustainable-living-apartment-habits`
4. `small-apartment-eco-upgrade-checklist`
5. `beginner-sustainable-living-checklist-renters`

Preliminary structure:

- **Cornerstone candidate:** 21 Easy Sustainable Habits on a Budget
- **Narrow waste support:** 15 Low Waste Lifestyle Tips
- **Utilities/constraint reposition or consolidation:** Sustainable Living in an Apartment: 10 Easy Habits
- **Paced implementation support:** 30-Day Small Apartment Eco-Upgrade Checklist
- **Shortest beginner entry:** Beginner Sustainable Living Checklist for Renters

Do not publish another broad renter-habits list before Search Console overlap, claims repair, and an owner consolidation decision.

### Decor overlap and proof risk

The four decor articles can remain distinct only when each serves a separate reader task:

- living-room guide = layout and room decisions;
- weekend checklist = bounded reset;
- budget decor guide = sourcing criteria and tradeoffs;
- before-and-after = documented transformation, or an explicitly hypothetical plan.

The before-and-after page currently does not fulfill a documented-proof role. Stronger inbound links would amplify the unsupported promise.

### Kitchen overlap

The four kitchen articles form a plausible cluster but repeat pantry visibility, containers, composting, food-waste reduction, and budget framing.

Preliminary separation:

- chef-habits article = food-use systems, after authority repair;
- pantry article = visibility, labeling, rotation, and narrow storage;
- budget swaps article = conditional cost and replacement decisions;
- tiny-apartment article = no-pantry and severe-space constraints, if retained.

Avoid treating all four as equivalent hubs or repeating exact-match “zero waste kitchen” anchors across every page.

## Preliminary cluster signals from the complete inventory

The current repository has the greatest depth in:

1. **Small-space zero-waste kitchen** — four articles, strong practical subtopics, high claims and overlap risk.
2. **Budget sustainable habits for renters** — five articles, strongest brand fit, very high cannibalization risk.
3. **Sustainable small-space decor** — four articles, very high visual potential, immediate transformation-proof risk.

These are inventory-strength observations, not final priority approval. Final selection requires direct audience evidence, dated query-family and SERP evidence, Search Console and GA4 data when available, brand fit, execution capacity, and cannibalization review.

## Immediate editorial priorities

1. Repair or reframe both articles using “Before & After.”
2. Remove or substantiate guaranteed savings and fixed-budget promises.
3. Source or neutralize chef and professional-practice authority.
4. Differentiate or consolidate the five renter-habit articles.
5. Decide whether the short tiny-kitchen article remains distinct.
6. Repair health, plant air-quality, food-storage, cleaning, lease, and universal product claims.
7. Implement contextual internal links only after destination promises are supportable.
8. Add truthful visual requirements to every transformation, layout, pantry, and decor brief.
