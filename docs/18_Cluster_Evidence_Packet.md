# ETLH Preliminary Cluster Evidence Packet

## Purpose

Evaluate the first three proposed ETLH search-topic clusters using only evidence currently available in the repository. This packet supports research and owner review; it does not select, approve, or lock a cluster.

Repository-based conclusions are preliminary editorial judgment, not verified market evidence. No search demand, traffic, ranking potential, conversion performance, or competitive opportunity is asserted here.

## Candidate 1: Small-space zero-waste kitchen

### Repository-supported coverage

| Existing article                                                        | Preliminary role                   | Repository observation                                                                       |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Zero Waste Pantry Organization for Small Apartments (Before & After)    | Supporting guide                   | Focuses on visibility, pantry zones, containers, rotation, and food-waste prevention.        |
| Low Waste Kitchen Tips: 11 Habits Chefs Use to Cut Food Waste           | Potential food-waste cornerstone   | Covers inventory, leftovers, flexible cooking, freezing, labeling, portions, and composting. |
| Zero Waste Kitchen on a Budget: 9 Simple Swaps That Actually Save Money | Potential budget-swaps cornerstone | Covers reusables, packaging, leftovers, composting, pantry setup, and energy use.            |
| Zero Waste Kitchen Ideas for Tiny Apartments                            | Supporting small-space primer      | Short guide focused on storage, apartment composting, and three pantry swaps.                |

### Content gaps

- A single orientation page that defines a realistic zero-waste kitchen for renters without promising literal zero waste.
- Food-storage safety, original-package label retention, date-label interpretation, and container suitability.
- Small-space compost options compared by access, odor management, cost, and local acceptance rules.
- Meal planning, leftovers, freezer management, and an "eat first" workflow as distinct practical guides.
- A renter-specific setup by kitchen constraint: no pantry, shared kitchen, very small freezer, or no local compost service.
- Evidence-backed cost comparisons that state assumptions instead of promising savings.

### Likely cornerstone role

Repository coverage supports a preliminary cornerstone concept such as **Small-Space Zero-Waste Kitchen: A Practical Renter's Guide**. It should orient readers across storage, food-waste prevention, reusables, compost access, budget tradeoffs, and safety, then route to narrower supporting articles.

The current chef-habits and budget-swaps articles are each broad enough to compete with that role. Their scopes must be narrowed and claims repaired before a new cornerstone is drafted.

### Cannibalization risk

**High.** All four articles repeat pantry visibility, reusable containers, composting, food-waste reduction, and budget framing. The short tiny-apartment guide substantially overlaps the three longer pieces. Distinct primary reader tasks and link relationships are required before expansion.

### Visual-production potential

**High, subject to truthful production.** Useful visuals include a measured cabinet layout, label-retention examples, an "eat first" shelf, freezer zones, compost-container options, and a documented pantry process. The existing before-and-after article cannot rely on illustrative imagery presented as a real transformation.

### Cross-channel usefulness

- Instagram: one Eco Step per kitchen system, such as moving reusable bags or creating an "eat first" shelf.
- Pinterest: measured pantry layouts, checklists, storage diagrams, and comparison pins.
- Email/download: a small-kitchen setup checklist or food-use rotation sheet.

### Claims and evidence risk

**High.** Current copy includes savings certainty, professional-chef attribution, environmental-impact language, "proven" waste-reduction claims, and a before-and-after presentation. Food safety, compost impact, energy use, and cost claims require authoritative sourcing or softer wording.

### External evidence still required

- Search Console query and landing-page data for all four existing articles.
- GA4 landing-page engagement and verified key-event data, if configured.
- Direct audience evidence about renter kitchen constraints and failed workarounds.
- Dated autocomplete and query-family records.
- Current competitor and SERP review for each distinct reader task.

## Candidate 2: Budget sustainable habits for renters

### Repository-supported coverage

| Existing article                                                           | Preliminary role                | Repository observation                                                                                                             |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 21 Easy Sustainable Habits on a Budget (Perfect for Renters)               | Potential cornerstone           | Broad renter-habits list spanning energy, water, waste, shopping, and routines.                                                    |
| 15 Low Waste Lifestyle Tips You Can Start This Week                        | Narrow supporting candidate     | Best differentiated when limited to waste, packaging, food, and reusables.                                                         |
| Sustainable Living in an Apartment: 10 Easy Habits That Don't Feel Extreme | Consolidate or reposition       | Broadly duplicates the 21-habits article; strongest possible distinction is renter-controlled utilities and apartment constraints. |
| 30-Day Small Apartment Eco-Upgrade Checklist (Under 100 Dollars)           | Supporting implementation plan  | Offers a sequenced checklist but does not currently substantiate the total budget.                                                 |
| Beginner Sustainable Living Checklist for Renters                          | Supporting beginner entry point | Short list with a low-guilt starting frame; overlaps the broader habit lists.                                                      |

### Content gaps

- A defensible distinction between no-cost, low-cost, and replacement-time actions.
- Renter constraints such as lease rules, shared utilities, landlord-controlled systems, and limited waste services.
- Decision guidance for when not to buy an "eco" replacement.
- Sourced utility, water, product-lifespan, and cost examples with location and assumption limits.
- Habit-maintenance systems tied to ETLH's friction-reduction principle.
- A consolidation plan for the three broad beginner/checklist articles.

### Likely cornerstone role

The 21-habits article is the strongest preliminary cornerstone candidate because it already carries the broadest renter and budget frame. A future refresh could organize actions by cost, effort, renter control, and likely maintenance burden while linking to narrower waste, utilities, kitchen, and shopping guides.

### Cannibalization risk

**Very high.** Five articles address substantially overlapping beginner, renter, checklist, budget, and easy-habit intent. Publishing more broad habit lists before consolidation would increase ambiguity for readers and search engines.

### Visual-production potential

**Medium to high.** Practical visuals could compare no-cost versus replacement-time actions, map renter-controlled systems, show one-week habit setups, or provide a verified budget worksheet. Avoid staged savings evidence or totals that are not supported by receipts and assumptions.

### Cross-channel usefulness

- Instagram: single Eco Steps and progress-over-perfection routines.
- Pinterest: renter checklists, cost/effort matrices, and 30-day sequences.
- Email/download: a choose-your-own starting plan grouped by budget and control.

### Claims and evidence risk

**High.** Current articles imply utility savings, environmental-footprint reductions, health benefits, fixed budget totals, and product durability. Those statements need sources, narrower conditions, or owner-confirmed evidence.

### External evidence still required

- Search Console query overlap and page performance for the five existing articles.
- GA4 landing-page engagement and verified next-step behavior.
- Audience research on the renter constraints that matter most.
- Dated query families separating beginner, budget, checklist, utilities, and low-waste intent.
- Current SERP review to determine whether consolidation or differentiation best serves each intent.

## Candidate 3: Sustainable small-space decor

### Repository-supported coverage

| Existing article                                                        | Preliminary role                       | Repository observation                                                                                            |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Sustainable Tiny Living Room: Layout and Styling Ideas for Small Spaces | Potential living-room cornerstone      | Broad layout and styling guide with room-specific scope.                                                          |
| Eco Friendly Small Apartment Checklist You Can Tackle in a Weekend      | Supporting reset checklist             | Mixes decor, cleaning, waste, energy, and pantry actions; requires a clearer primary job.                         |
| Sustainable Small Apartment Decor: Before & After on a Budget           | Supporting proof article, after repair | Currently presents a real sub-$300 transformation without repository evidence or matching before/after visuals.   |
| Eco Friendly Small Apartment Decor on a Budget                          | Supporting budget guide                | Short article on thrifting, plants, and renter-safe changes; several absolute recommendations need qualification. |

### Content gaps

- Measured layout examples for common small-room shapes and circulation constraints.
- Renter-safe guidance that distinguishes removable products from lease-dependent changes.
- A materials framework covering durability, repair, secondhand condition, indoor exposure, and disposal tradeoffs without broad "eco-friendly" labels.
- Truthful visual case studies with source, budget, dimensions, and limitations.
- Small-space storage guidance that does not encourage unnecessary container purchases.
- Lighting guidance separated from unsupported air-quality or health claims.

### Likely cornerstone role

The living-room layout article is the strongest current cornerstone candidate within this cluster. A broader apartment-decor cornerstone may eventually be useful, but repository evidence does not yet show whether readers want a room-specific hub or a whole-apartment guide.

### Cannibalization risk

**Medium to high.** The living-room article can remain distinct, but the weekend checklist, budget decor guide, and before-and-after article overlap around low-cost styling, plants, thrifting, and renter-safe changes. The before-and-after article needs evidence repair before it can serve as proof content.

### Visual-production potential

**Very high.** Floor plans, measured furniture footprints, lighting layers, secondhand evaluation details, storage use, and documented room sequences could add genuine value. This cluster also carries the highest risk of decorative or generated images being mistaken for documented transformations.

### Cross-channel usefulness

- Instagram: one-surface resets, room-angle comparisons, and renter-safe micro changes.
- Pinterest: measured layouts, room checklists, and visual decision guides.
- Email/download: a small-room planning sheet with dimensions, needs, and purchase pauses.

### Claims and evidence risk

**High.** Current copy presents a real before-and-after project, a fixed budget, a 30-percent size perception, better air quality from plants, sweeping marketplace comparisons, and extreme plant-survival language. These require documented evidence, qualification, or reframing.

### External evidence still required

- Search Console queries and landing-page performance for the four decor articles.
- GA4 engagement and verified conversion or next-step data.
- Audience evidence about layout, clutter, rental restrictions, budget, and decor-purchase frustrations.
- Dated autocomplete and query-family evidence by room and constraint.
- Current competitor/SERP review, including the visual formats that dominate results and whether they provide measured or documented proof.

## Evidence still required before selection

### Search Console

- Query and page exports for a consistent complete period.
- Impressions, clicks, CTR, average position, and query overlap by existing article.
- Index status and canonical status for candidate cornerstone and supporting pages.
- Enough observations to avoid decisions based on very small or privacy-filtered samples.

Owner account access is required.

### GA4

- Users, engaged sessions, engagement time, and landing-page behavior for the existing articles.
- Verified key events only after event definitions and success states are documented.
- Source/medium context for organic search and cross-channel visits.

Owner account access is required. Current repository evidence does not contain a sufficient article-level performance export.

### Audience research

- Multiple independent first-person problem records per candidate.
- Living context, renter restrictions, budget constraints, attempted solutions, and failure points.
- Clear separation between isolated anecdotes and repeated patterns.

### Autocomplete and query families

- Dated, location/language-aware query-family records.
- Distinct informational tasks rather than cosmetic keyword variants.
- Questions and modifiers that reveal room, budget, rental, storage, waste-service, or routine constraints.

### Competitor and SERP review

- Dated review of dominant intent, result format, authority, evidence quality, freshness, and small-space relevance.
- Gaps ETLH can address with a supportable practical outcome.
- No ranking or traffic prediction based solely on a weak result set.
