# ETLH Publishing Cadence Operating Plan

## Purpose

Provide a realistic, low-friction operating cadence for a solo owner across the ETLH blog, Instagram, Pinterest, refresh work, analytics review, and internal linking.

This is an operational recommendation, not an owner decision or a commitment to a fixed number of weekly articles. The owner selects a level based on available time, evidence, production capacity, and review needs.

## Operating principles

1. Quality, evidence, and consistency take priority over volume.
2. One useful source can support several channel packages, but each package must fit its platform and retain the source article's evidence boundaries.
3. New production must not crowd out claims repair, refreshes, internal links, or measurement.
4. Work in complete packages. Avoid accumulating half-drafted articles or visuals without a defined destination and review path.
5. Reduce the level when the backlog, owner-review queue, or evidence burden exceeds available capacity.
6. Do not publish merely to fill a calendar slot.

## Unit of work

A **content cycle** is the smallest complete operating unit. Depending on the chosen scope, it may be a new article, a substantial refresh, or a focused repair.

A complete cycle can include:

1. evidence packet and search-content brief;
2. article draft or refresh plan;
3. claims, metadata, image, accessibility, and internal-link review;
4. repository validation and publication approval;
5. Pinterest and Instagram packages when they add value;
6. indexing, distribution, and measurement initialization;
7. later performance review using a complete comparison window.

Not every cycle needs every channel. Channel packaging should follow reader usefulness and available assets, not a quota.

## Operating levels

### Level 1: Minimum sustainable

Use when owner time is constrained, research evidence is incomplete, or the repair backlog is more important than new production.

**Primary commitment:** keep one active content cycle moving to completion without creating parallel production debt.

| Workstream       | Recommended pattern                                                                                                                                                 |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Blog             | One article, refresh, or claims-repair cycle at a time. Choose the next publication window only after the current cycle clears review.                              |
| Instagram        | Adapt one proven idea from the active cycle or maintain the approved queue. Skip a slot when visuals, caption, first comment, hashtags, or alt text are incomplete. |
| Pinterest        | Produce pins only for a verified destination with an honest visual promise and final URL.                                                                           |
| Refresh work     | Reserve at least one cycle out of every few completed cycles for claims, freshness, consolidation, or proof repair.                                                 |
| Analytics        | Short weekly operational check when access exists; one complete monthly review.                                                                                     |
| Internal linking | Review inbound and outbound links during every article or refresh cycle; perform a small cluster check monthly.                                                     |

**Best fit:** early-stage measurement, one-person production, or periods with limited visual capacity.

### Level 2: Standard

Use when the owner can sustain a predictable production and review rhythm without carrying unresolved drafts or publishing unsupported claims.

**Primary commitment:** maintain one main article/refresh lane and one batched channel-packaging lane.

| Workstream       | Recommended pattern                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Blog             | Maintain one researched article or substantial refresh in production and one evidence packet in preparation. Do not move the second item into drafting until the first clears review. |
| Instagram        | Batch a small set of packages from approved article ideas, Eco Steps, or completed refreshes. Publish at the owner's chosen frequency.                                                |
| Pinterest        | Batch titles, descriptions, alt text, and visual variants for approved destinations after article metadata and URLs are final.                                                        |
| Refresh work     | Alternate expansion with repair: after a new article cycle, select a refresh, claims repair, or consolidation task when evidence indicates one.                                       |
| Analytics        | Weekly anomaly and operations review; monthly performance and landing-page review; quarterly cluster review.                                                                          |
| Internal linking | Update links as part of each publication, then review one active cluster after each completed content cycle.                                                                          |

**Best fit:** steady solo-owner operation with established templates, reliable asset production, and enough evidence to prioritize work.

### Level 3: Accelerated

Use only for a bounded campaign, a well-supported cluster build, or a period with additional production/review capacity. It is not the default steady state.

**Primary commitment:** run multiple controlled lanes while preserving independent evidence, review, and publication gates.

| Workstream       | Recommended pattern                                                                                                                           |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Blog             | One article in final review, one in drafting, and one evidence packet in research. No lane advances without its own evidence and claims gate. |
| Instagram        | Batch multiple approved packages, with visual variety and the full caption/hashtags/first-comment/alt-text workflow.                          |
| Pinterest        | Produce multiple creative variants only for live or fully approved destinations; record each destination and package identifier.              |
| Refresh work     | Maintain a dedicated repair lane so new production does not postpone claims, proof, or cannibalization work.                                  |
| Analytics        | Weekly operational and content-trend review, monthly decision review, and an end-of-campaign assessment using comparable windows.             |
| Internal linking | Run a cluster-level link pass before and after each publication group, including sibling differentiation and orphan checks.                   |

**Entry conditions:** approved cluster, completed evidence packets, clear owner capacity, available visual production, manageable review queue, and no unresolved high-risk claims in the affected cluster.

**Exit condition:** return to standard or minimum when quality gates slip, review queues grow, measurement cannot keep up, or owner capacity changes.

## Choosing a level

Use the lowest level that can be sustained without skipped evidence, accessibility, claims, consent, or validation work.

| Question                                            | Minimum                      | Standard                       | Accelerated                                |
| --------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------ |
| How many active editorial lanes?                    | 1                            | 1 active plus 1 prepared       | Up to 3 controlled stages                  |
| Is a cluster owner-approved and evidence-supported? | Not required for repair work | Preferred for new cluster work | Required                                   |
| Can visuals be produced and reviewed truthfully?    | As available                 | Predictably                    | At campaign volume                         |
| Can the owner review decisions promptly?            | Limited                      | Reliable                       | Scheduled capacity                         |
| Is measurement mature enough to guide sequencing?   | Baseline only is acceptable  | Monthly comparisons available  | Campaign and cluster comparisons available |

No level establishes a mandatory publication count. The owner may keep the same level while changing the calendar frequency.

## Suggested cycle rhythm

### Weekly operational block

When account access exists:

- check site, deployment, indexing, and analytics anomalies;
- move the active content cycle to its next gate;
- review the owner-decision queue;
- confirm the next scheduled social package is complete;
- add or validate internal links for work nearing publication.

This is an operations check, not a requirement to publish weekly.

### Monthly review block

- record Search Console, GA4, Pinterest, and Instagram metrics using complete windows;
- review active landing pages and cluster pages;
- choose refresh, expand, consolidate, CTR, or internal-link actions using documented evidence;
- review the content and owner-approval queues;
- select or revise the operating level for the next period.

### Quarterly planning block

- review cluster direction and cannibalization;
- revisit publishing capacity and maintenance burden;
- review claims, visual-proof, and freshness backlogs;
- confirm whether targets or business priorities require an owner decision;
- retire no content or channel without a separate evidence-backed decision.

## Work that can be batched

- repository inventory and overlap checks;
- source collection and research-record normalization;
- query-family and SERP review for closely related candidate topics;
- metadata drafts after article scope is stable;
- internal-link candidate lists and anchor variation;
- Instagram captions, hashtags, first comments, and alt-text drafts;
- Pinterest titles, descriptions, alt text, and visual briefs;
- image resizing, compression, naming, and export verification;
- scheduled metric exports and manual dashboard entry;
- repository validation across a set of documentation-only changes when each concern still receives a focused PR.

Batching must not erase article-specific evidence, claims, alt text, or destination review.

## Work that should remain item-specific

- final reader problem and search intent;
- claims and source verification;
- before-and-after or transformation proof;
- article promise, scope, and exclusions;
- final metadata and structured-data eligibility;
- final image-to-copy truthfulness review;
- publication and production verification;
- refresh, consolidation, redirect, or deletion decisions.

## Owner review required

- final cluster selection and priority;
- publication level and calendar commitments;
- new article or major refresh approval where the workflow requires it;
- brand direction, named identity, credentials, or personal-experience claims;
- legal, privacy-policy, affiliate, or regulated-content decisions;
- purchases, paid tools, contractors, or new account configuration;
- conversion definitions, business targets, and campaign priorities;
- consolidation, redirects, unpublishing, or deletion.

## Low-friction workflow controls

Maintain four short queues:

1. **Evidence queue:** candidate topics waiting for research.
2. **Production queue:** approved briefs moving through draft, visual, and validation work.
3. **Repair queue:** claims, proof, refresh, consolidation, and internal-link work.
4. **Owner queue:** decisions that cannot proceed autonomously.

Limit work in progress according to the selected operating level. A blocked item should move to the owner queue or evidence queue rather than occupying the production lane indefinitely.

## Definition of a healthy cadence

The cadence is healthy when:

- completed work is more common than abandoned drafts;
- evidence and claims checks remain intact;
- social packages point to useful, verified destinations;
- accessibility, technical, and consent gates continue to pass;
- refresh and internal-link work receive regular capacity;
- measurements are recorded without invented conclusions;
- the owner-review queue remains visible and manageable;
- the selected level feels repeatable for the next reporting period.

## Recommended starting point

Use **minimum sustainable** until the current claims, transformation-proof, and overlap risks are triaged. Move to **standard** only after the owner approves a cluster direction and the production/review workflow demonstrates a repeatable complete cycle. Treat **accelerated** as a temporary, evidence-backed campaign mode.
