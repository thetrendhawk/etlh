# ETLH Site Asset Inventory

## Purpose

Create a repository-grounded record of every image asset currently used, referenced, or publicly addressable by the ETLH application. This inventory separates visual illustration from documentary evidence and identifies the highest-risk replacements without changing public assets.

## Scope and evidence boundary

Audited on July 19, 2026 against `main` at `2da9d9d`:

- `src/assets/` and `public/favicon.svg`;
- all image imports and image markup in `src/`;
- all 14 records in `src/lib/posts.ts`;
- the public room-reset override in `src/lib/postPresentation.ts`;
- homepage, about, blog, article, category, resource, and root metadata routes;
- current asset, publishing, design, claims, responsive-image, and visibility documentation.

Dimensions and byte sizes were read from the source files. Visual descriptions and embedded-text findings come from direct inspection of the files. No source, license, prompt, model, photographer, creator, release, or generation record is stored with the raster assets. A generated-looking image is therefore recorded as **provenance unknown**, not asserted to be AI-generated, unless the file itself supplies evidence such as the visible Midjourney mark in `post-decor.jpg`.

This is a documentation audit. It does not establish traffic, conversion, ranking, or audience value; generate or replace images; publish content; or select a topic cluster.

## Rating key

- **P0 — remove from public presentation:** materially misleading evidence, false alt text, obsolete destination, or unsupported embedded claim.
- **P1 — replace next:** substantial truthfulness, accessibility, reuse, or reader-value problem.
- **P2 — improve in a planned refresh:** usable illustration with provenance, crop, specificity, or brand-fit limitations.
- **P3 — retain and maintain:** fit for current purpose; preserve source and usage records.

Risk values are `high`, `medium`, `low`, or `none observed`. `Unknown` means the repository does not contain enough evidence to decide.

## Public delivery model

- A post image is reused as its article hero, article card, Open Graph image, Twitter image, and Article structured-data image.
- Post cards appear on `/blog`, the matching category route, related-article sections, and, for the first six source records, the homepage.
- Article heroes use a 2:3 container. Cards use a 16:10 crop. Pinterest graphics therefore lose substantial text and context on cards.
- The homepage hero is also the homepage social image. The same file is reused on `/about` and in its social metadata.
- Category routes have no category-specific hero. Their only imagery comes from article cards.
- The root fallback Open Graph/Twitter image is externally hosted, not stored in this repository. Its provenance and durability are outside this inventory and should be brought under repository or controlled-host ownership in a separate implementation.
- No downloadable PDF, worksheet, or other downloadable binary resource is stored in the repository. `/resources` currently offers navigation, not files.
- No standalone production logo source exists. The favicon is the only reusable brand asset; nine Pinterest graphics contain flattened logo treatments that cannot serve as an authoritative logo source.

## Cross-cutting findings

### 1. Pinterest graphics are being used as website content images

Nine portrait graphics (A08–A16) are channel artwork, not suitable universal website imagery. They combine article titles, small body copy, icons, claims, logos, and photographic or generated backgrounds. Reusing them for article heroes, 16:10 cards, Open Graph, Twitter, and structured data creates five problems:

1. important text is inaccessible or duplicated;
2. 16:10 card crops remove context and create arbitrary fragments;
3. alt text describes selected background objects rather than the actual infographic;
4. social and article roles cannot be revised independently;
5. obsolete or unsupported copy is replicated anywhere the article URL is shared.

Website heroes and cards should use a purpose-built, text-free visual. Pinterest and Instagram adaptations should be separate derived assets with their own validation and destination record.

### 2. Every stored Pinterest graphic carries an obsolete identity

A08–A13 and A16 visibly use `ecotinylivinghub.thrwds.com`; A14–A15 omit the footer domain but still use the same legacy promotional system. The authoritative public host is `ecotinylivinghub.com`. None of these graphics should be newly distributed in current form.

### 3. Unsupported proof and outcome framing is concentrated in five assets

- A08, A14, and A16 use before/after framing without documentary provenance.
- A08 promises a fixed “Under $300” result.
- A14–A16 promise savings, health, environmental, time, stress, safety, or nutrition outcomes without stated assumptions or evidence.
- Generated, staged, or illustrative visuals cannot substantiate a transformation, price, savings, professional authority, or environmental result.

### 4. Alt text is inaccurate across most article graphics

A07’s second use and A12 are plainly false descriptions. A08–A16 generally describe a photographic background while omitting the dominant text and meaning. For linked cards whose adjacent title already provides the destination, a concise description or empty alt may be more useful than repeating article keywords. Informational diagrams require a nearby accessible text equivalent; alt text should not reproduce an entire graphic.

### 5. Provenance and licensing records are absent

No raster asset has a repository record for creator, source, license, purchase, release, generation method, model, prompt, edit history, or approval. A06 visibly identifies Midjourney, but no license or generation record accompanies it. Until provenance is recorded, these assets should be treated as limited-use illustrations, not documentary evidence or reusable brand masters.

### 6. Category and downloadable experiences have asset gaps

- Category routes have no category hero, diagram, or orientation graphic; repeated article cards provide the entire visual experience.
- Intentional Living has no homepage category card because the homepage mapping includes only three categories.
- No downloadable resource files exist, despite content planning references to a Friction Finder and the resource route’s future role.
- No authoritative logo master, Open Graph master, social template source, original-photo library, or provenance register exists.

## Replacement sequence

1. **Correct P0 public truthfulness/accessibility issues:** A06, the false-alt A07 use, and live A09–A16 article presentations.
2. **Decouple channel art from website art:** introduce separate article-hero/card and social-derivative fields or a documented naming/folder convention before creating replacements.
3. **Remove dormant unsafe dependency:** delete A08 from the public source graph once retention needs are decided; the repaired article already uses A03.
4. **Create article-specific useful visuals:** prioritize pantry zoning, food-use rotation, small-room layout, and starter/checklist tools over decorative volume.
5. **Establish provenance:** record source, creator/tool, license, generation status, permitted uses, approval, and documentary/illustrative classification for every new asset.
6. **Create controlled brand masters:** approved logo source, default Open Graph image, and channel templates without relying on flattened legacy graphics.
7. **Improve lower-risk generic imagery:** A01–A05 after the misleading article graphics are removed.

This sequence does not select a topic cluster. Final production order should be reconciled with the separate Perplexity research before cluster-led expansion.

## Definition of done for the future implementation program

- No public image implies documentary proof without provenance and supporting records.
- No website hero/card contains obsolete domains, unsupported prices, guaranteed savings, before/after proof, or unqualified health/environmental outcomes.
- Every alt value accurately describes the asset’s function in context.
- Website, Pinterest, Instagram, Open Graph, and downloadable roles use independently controlled assets.
- Each asset has source, license, creator/tool, approval, version, and permitted-use records.
- All heroes and cards pass desktop and mobile crop review without relying on embedded text.
- Informational graphics have an accessible text equivalent.
- Repository asset budgets, responsive delivery, CI, and exact-head preview validation pass.
