# ETLH Article Asset Gap Map

## Purpose

Identify the smallest set of useful visual and downloadable aids that could improve the reader experience across all 14 current ETLH articles. This map prioritizes explanation, decision support, and follow-through over decorative image quantity.

## Scope and boundary

Audited on July 19, 2026 against `main` at `2da9d9d`, including all post records, public presentation overrides, article/card rendering, claims inventory, internal-linking map, design system, and current repository assets.

This document does not:

- select or lock a topic cluster;
- claim search demand, traffic, conversion, audience preference, cost, savings, or environmental effect;
- generate images or final downloads;
- rewrite or publish public article copy;
- treat a proposed asset as approved evidence.

External Perplexity research must be reviewed before cluster-led production order is finalized.

## Priority key

- **P0 — trust/accessibility repair:** current visual is materially misleading, contains unsupported embedded claims, or has false alt text.
- **P1 — high reader value:** a focused diagram, table, checklist, or worksheet would materially improve understanding or execution.
- **P2 — planned enhancement:** useful after higher-risk gaps and evidence needs are resolved.
- **P3 — optional:** decorative or channel adaptation only; do not prioritize over instructional assets.

## Portfolio findings

- Nine articles use text-heavy Pinterest graphics as website heroes, cards, and metadata images. These channel assets are cropped, inaccessible, and often contain obsolete or unsupported copy.
- Two articles share `SustainablePin1.webp` despite different reader tasks. Two checklist articles share `post-checklist.jpg`, and one use has false alt text.
- Most articles contain list-based guidance but no visual hierarchy, decision aid, or worksheet that helps the reader adapt advice to a real small space.
- Three useful resource connections recur across the portfolio: an Eco Step starter sheet, a food-use rotation sheet, and a small-room reset planning sheet.
- Original photography would add value only when it documents a real setup, sequence, measurement, or tradeoff. Generic staged “eco” still lifes would not close the evidence gap.

## Definition of done for a future article-asset implementation

- The asset resolves a named reader task rather than adding decoration.
- The article's current claims and role are stable enough to support production.
- No generated or illustrative visual is presented as documentary proof.
- Website, Open Graph, Pinterest, Instagram, and download roles are intentionally exported and validated.
- Alt, caption, long description, table, or HTML equivalent is complete.
- Claims, measurements, costs, safety notes, permissions, sources, and provenance are recorded.
- Desktop/mobile crops and repository validation pass.
- Owner approval is recorded for documentary evidence, paid/licensed assets, consolidation-sensitive articles, and public downloads.
