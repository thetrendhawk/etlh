1. “Eat-first” is an organizational label, not a safety label.
2. Look only at what is currently present.
3. Group one small area; do not empty the appliance.
4. For food decisions, consult the package, manufacturer, FoodKeeper, FDA, or USDA/FSIS.
5. Use, freeze where appropriate, retain, seek guidance, discard, ask for help, or stop.

**Caption:** A bounded visibility check can be smaller than a refrigerator overhaul. It does not determine whether food is safe, fresh, or spoiled, and it does not promise a food-waste or convenience outcome.
