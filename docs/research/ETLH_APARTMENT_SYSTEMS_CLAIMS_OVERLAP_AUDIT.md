# ETLH Apartment Systems Claims and Overlap Audit

**Route:** `/blog/sustainable-living-apartment-easy-habits`  
**Audit date:** 2026-07-20  
**Decision:** Differentiate and repair in place; retain the URL.

## Independent-purpose decision

The current article overlaps heavily with the renter checklist, the 20-option renter-aware habits article, and the low-waste practices article. All four currently repeat use-what-you-own, reusable carry items, cleaning and food-storage caveats, and local-service constraints. The current article also adds unsupported bill, waste, water, energy, emissions, health, comfort, ease, affordability, and habit-success outcomes.

An independent reader purpose remains defensible if the article stops being a fourth general habit list and becomes an apartment-systems control map: identify what a renter can change, what requires household agreement, what belongs with a landlord or building, and what depends on utility, municipal, or purchase access. That decision framework is not the purpose of the three comparison pages.

## Overlap matrix

| Page | Existing purpose | Overlap | Differentiation boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Beginner renter checklist | Five low-pressure starter options | use-what-you-own, one reversible action, lease/access checks | This article maps responsibility and escalation paths rather than starter actions |
| 20 renter-aware habits | Broad options across energy, water, cleaning, shopping, food, and waste | nearly every current section and item | This article uses a small set of control classes, not a numbered habit inventory |
| 15 low-waste practices | low-waste observation, reuse, carrying, food storage, local end-of-life | reusables, repair, food, compost, local disposal | This article treats waste as one service-dependent system among several |

## Claim disposition

| Claim family | Disposition |
| --- | --- |
| savings, bill reduction, “biggest footprint,” emissions, waste, water, energy outcomes | remove outcome language; describe decisions and dependencies only |
| health, calm, comfort, productivity, stress | remove outcome promises; retain comfort, health, and accessibility as constraints |
| easy, simple, affordable, realistic, low-cost | remove universal framing; use “may fit” and explicit constraints |
| fixed timelines, habit formation, “add up quickly” | remove |
| replacement-first, LEDs, natural materials, reusables, secondhand | make existing-item, repair, borrow, and access checks primary; purchases are optional and conditional |
| landlord, utility, municipal, manufacturer guidance | retain as control boundaries and verification steps; no legal or technical conclusion beyond the cited instruction |

## Asset and metadata findings

`SustainablePin1.webp` is also used by `why-life-feels-harder-than-it-needs-to-sometimes`, so it cannot be removed globally in this slice. The apartment article’s source metadata will be aligned to the existing `hero-apartment.jpg` presentation image and its illustrative alt text. This removes a source/presentation mismatch without claiming the image is evidence of an outcome.

## Link and regression plan

The repaired article will link to the renter checklist for a starter path, the 20-option article for broader option browsing, and the low-waste article for waste-specific detail. Regression coverage will assert the four control classes, the absence of the retired claim families, distinct metadata/headings, source/presentation image consistency, and those links. Rendered accessibility coverage will include the route on desktop and mobile.

## Evidence limits

No analytics, search-volume, ranking-difficulty, savings, emissions, waste, health, or habit-formation evidence is available for this audit. The differentiated article is an editorial decision map, not a measured intervention or demand claim.
