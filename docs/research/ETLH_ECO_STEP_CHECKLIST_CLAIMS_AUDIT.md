# Small-apartment eco step checklist claims audit

Audited: 2026-07-19

Route: `/blog/small-apartment-eco-upgrade-checklist`

Decision: repair this article next and preserve its URL and optional 30-day pacing structure.

## Selection rationale

The claims inventory identifies this as the next explicit repair priority after the completed proof, pantry, budget, and chef-authority work. The source record still promises a full plan under $100, universal renter safety, no landlord permission, and predictable results from fixed daily/weekly routines. Its cleaning, laundry, compost, storage, adhesive, and purchasing directions omit important surface, product, appliance, lease, accessibility, local-service, and household limits.

## Evidence available

- CDC cleaning guidance: ordinary cleaning and disinfecting are different tasks; label directions control; cleaning products and chemicals should not be mixed.
- EPA Safer Choice guidance: vague “green” or “eco-safe” labels are not proof; certified product information is a more reviewable option when a purchase is needed.
- The repaired ETLH pantry, kitchen-swaps, food-use, and apartment-composting articles provide package, appliance, food-safety, destination, local-rule, no-purchase, and household boundaries.
- The claims inventory and intent map support retaining an optional paced checklist while removing the fixed budget.

## Evidence missing

- No itemized, dated, location-specific cost record supports a total under $100.
- No evidence establishes that 30 days, one action per day, or a weekly routine improves adherence or outcomes for ETLH readers.
- No evidence establishes universal renter permission, surface safety, product lifespan, savings, waste reduction, or environmental impact.
- No source supports the current market-prevalence comparison about other articles costing $50 or assuming homeownership.
