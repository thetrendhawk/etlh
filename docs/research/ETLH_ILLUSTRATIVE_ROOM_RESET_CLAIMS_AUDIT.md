# Illustrative Room-Reset Claims Audit

**Route:** `/blog/sustainable-small-apartment-decor-before-after`  
**Audit date:** 2026-07-20  
**Owner decision:** No original notes, photos, receipts, measurements, dates, or other evidence support a real transformation. Preserve the URL, remove documented before/after framing, and present the page as illustrative planning.

## Reframed purpose

The page now demonstrates how a reader might organize one hypothetical room reset: inspect the current room, separate fixed constraints from movable items, test reversible changes, and pause before buying. It is not a case study, proof of an outcome, sourcing guide, deadline checklist, layout authority, or apartment-wide control map.

## Overlap boundary

- The weekend reset is the shorter one-room inspect-and-stop sequence.
- The living-room layout page handles measurement, furniture footprints, and pathway testing.
- The decor sourcing page handles purchase criteria after a verified need exists.
- The apartment-systems page handles renter, household, building, utility, and municipal dependencies.

This route is distinct because it shows how several planning decisions can be organized in one hypothetical scenario without claiming that the depicted room or result is real.

## Claims disposition

Removed or narrowed all real-project, before/after, measured-result, budget, savings, environmental, air-quality, wellbeing, productivity, visual-size, durability, universal-rule, replacement-first, and result-implying image claims. Added explicit illustrative disclosure, no-purchase-first guidance, lease/building/manufacturer/safety/accessibility/household/child/pet/allergy/physical-ability boundaries, and valid restore/no-change outcomes.

## Limitations

The scenario is editorially illustrative. No project provenance, measured spatial outcome, savings, environmental impact, or search-demand conclusion is implied.
