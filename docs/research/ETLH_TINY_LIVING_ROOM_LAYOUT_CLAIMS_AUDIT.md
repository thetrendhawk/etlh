# Tiny Living Room Layout Claims and Overlap Audit

**Route:** `/blog/sustainable-tiny-living-room-layout-ideas`  
**Audit date:** 2026-07-20  
**Status:** Repaired in place; URL preserved.

## Overlap analysis

The original page combined layout examples, furniture sourcing, sustainability labels, plants, styling, and visual-outcome promises. That overlapped the weekend reset, decor sourcing, illustrative room-reset, and apartment-systems pages. It has a defensible independent purpose when narrowed to planning and testing a small living-room arrangement before buying or installing anything.

| Related route | Shared material | Boundary retained |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `/blog/eco-friendly-small-apartment-weekend-checklist` | One-room reset, clear surfaces, reversible changes | This page is measurement, footprint, pathways, and layout testing; the reset page is the shorter inspect-and-stop sequence. |
| `/blog/sustainable-small-apartment-decor-before-after` | Furniture, storage, lighting, and styling | This page is not a documented transformation or before/after result. |
| `/blog/eco-friendly-small-apartment-decor-budget` | Materials, secondhand options, and purchase decisions | This page postpones sourcing until a verified layout need exists; the sourcing guide handles tradeoffs. |
| `/blog/sustainable-living-apartment-easy-habits` | Renter, building, household, utility, and access constraints | This page applies those constraints to one room’s footprint and pathways, not apartment-wide systems. |

The original “feel bigger,” “actually work,” cozy, sustainable, and air-quality framing was not independently supportable. Consolidation is not recommended because a measurement-and-testing guide answers a distinct planning problem and can route readers to the narrower reset and sourcing pages.

## Claims disposition

| Finding | Classification | Repair |
| --- | --- | --- |
| “Feel bigger,” open, calm, cozy, productive, or better-flow outcomes | Remove | Use observable tests and room-specific tradeoffs; no spatial or wellbeing promise. |
| “Works in most rooms” and universal layout rules | Remove | Offer sketches, tape outlines, and reversible comparisons instead. |
| Natural, secondhand, compact, durable, repairable, or sustainable furniture claims | Narrow | Treat labels as questions to verify; compare footprint, care, fit, safety, and actual need. |
| Plants improve air quality; rugs, mirrors, color, or lighting change measured space | Remove | Keep visual choices optional and illustrative only. |
| Purchase-first and replacement advice | Replace with process guidance | Lead with current furniture and no-purchase tests; replace only for a documented functional need. |
| Clearance, egress, electrical, heater, anchoring, ventilation, window, child, pet, and accessibility advice | Narrow / source boundary | Defer to local/building/manufacturer guidance and preserve access and safety. |

## Asset review

`DecorPin3.webp` was a text-heavy promotional comparison graphic with unsupported air, mood, health, waste, and budget claims. It was used only by this route, so it is preserved as a source artifact but no longer imported or emitted. The route now uses the existing illustrative `hero-apartment.jpg` with matching source and presentation alt text; the image does not imply a documented result.

## Differentiation conclusion

The repaired title, metadata, headings, and body are not interchangeable with the reset checklist, sourcing guide, transformation page, or apartment-systems map. Internal links explain those adjacent paths without treating any page as proof of an outcome.

## Limitations

No universal measurement, clearance, savings, environmental, demand, or spatial-outcome claim was invented. Any jurisdiction-specific safety or accessibility requirement remains outside this general editorial guide and must be checked against applicable authority, building rules, and manufacturer instructions.
