## Selection rationale

Of the two unrepaired kitchen articles, this page has the broader reader-risk surface. It combines guaranteed savings and waste outcomes with purchase recommendations, blanket food-transfer advice, fixed household routines, universal water and local-service assumptions, and unsafe advice to let hot food cool before refrigeration. The chef-habits article still needs a separate repair, but its remaining risks are narrower and it is the intended canonical home for food-use guidance.

## Evidence available

- US EPA, [Preventing Wasted Food at Home](https://www.epa.gov/recycle/preventing-wasted-food-home): supports checking existing food, planning for meals actually eaten, buying usable quantities, proper storage, and treating bulk as beneficial only when food will be used.
- US FDA, [Refrigerator Thermometers: Cold Facts about Food Safety](https://www.fda.gov/food/buy-store-serve-safe-food/refrigerator-thermometers-cold-facts-about-food-safety): says leftovers should be refrigerated within two hours and hot food can go into the refrigerator; large amounts should be divided into shallow containers.
- USDA FSIS, [Food Product Dating](https://www.fsis.usda.gov/guidelines/2019-0022): explains the scope and meaning of food date labels; it does not support treating every printed date as an expiration/discard date.
- ENERGY STAR, [Learn About LED Lighting](https://www.energystar.gov/products/learn-about-led-lighting): supports the narrow statement that well-designed LEDs are more efficient and typically last longer than incandescent lighting.
- Existing ETLH repairs supply the pantry package-information safeguards and the compost guide's destination-first, local-rule, no-purchase, and accessibility-aware paths.

## Evidence missing

- No household-specific price, utility, purchase-frequency, lifespan, laundering, or disposal data supports a savings or payback promise.
- No comparative lifecycle evidence supports “high impact” or universally lower waste.
- No evidence establishes that bulk is cheaper or less wasteful for every reader.
- No evidence establishes universal access to safe tap water, suitable filters, recycling, organics collection, laundry, a freezer, or renter-controlled lighting.
- No named, verified creator or professional source supports the authority claims.
