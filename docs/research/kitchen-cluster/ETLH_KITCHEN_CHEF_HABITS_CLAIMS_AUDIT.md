# Chef-habits article claims audit

Audited: 2026-07-19

Route: `/blog/low-waste-kitchen-tips-chef-habits`

Decision: repair this article next and preserve its URL.

## Selection rationale

The other three kitchen articles now have focused body-claims repairs. This is the highest-risk remaining kitchen article because it combines unsupported chef and restaurant authority with promised waste, savings, nutrient, food-quality, and emotional outcomes. It also gives broad directions for produce, trimmings, leftovers, freezing, portions, storage, and composting without consistently preserving food-safety, package, appliance, household, accessibility, or local-program limits.

## Evidence available

- FDA, **Are You Storing Food Safely?**: prompt refrigeration, temperatures, airflow, label directions, covered storage, spoilage limits, and the distinction between date labels and food safety.
- FDA, **7 Tips for Cleaning Fruits, Vegetables**: running-water washing, clean hands, damaged-area removal, firm-produce brushing, drying, and refrigerated storage where required.
- USDA FSIS, **Leftovers and Food Safety**: safe cooking, prompt refrigeration, shallow-container cooling, covered storage, safe thawing, and reheating boundaries.
- USDA FSIS, **Freezing and Food Safety**: freezing does not destroy all microbes, quality varies, safe prior handling remains necessary, and package directions remain controlling.
- EPA composting guidance and the repaired ETLH apartment-composting guide: facility acceptance varies, so destination rules control food-scrap collection and staging.
- Existing ETLH pantry and budget-swaps repairs: preserve package directions, allergens, lot codes, storage directions, manufacturer limits, local conditions, and household variation.

## Evidence missing

- No evidence verifies that these practices were supplied by chefs or are standard across professional kitchens.
- No study in the repository establishes waste reduction, savings, meal quality, nutrient, satisfaction, or environmental outcomes for these 11 practices in ETLH's audience.
- No evidence ranks these practices or shows that one schedule, portion, container, appliance, or compost route fits every household.
- The kitchen-cluster research contains no measured search volume, ranking difficulty, or representative prevalence data.
