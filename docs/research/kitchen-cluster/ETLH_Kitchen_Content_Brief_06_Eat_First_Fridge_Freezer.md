# ETLH kitchen content brief 06 — Eat-first fridge and freezer check

| Field | Brief |
| --- | --- |
| Working title | An Eat-First Fridge and Freezer Check for Small Apartments |
| Route | `/blog/eat-first-fridge-freezer-small-apartment` |
| Reader problem | Already-purchased refrigerated and frozen food can become difficult to see before the next meal or shopping decision. |
| Independent purpose | Make a small group of existing foods visible for one bounded decision check; not a meal plan, cleanout challenge, storage-time chart, nutrition article, makeover, or safety routine. |
| Intent | Informational: organize visibility while separating an organizational label from food-safety judgment. |
| Evidence | USDA/FSIS food-safety basics; FSIS freezing guidance; FDA date-label/storage guidance; FoodKeeper only for food-specific details; package, manufacturer, and appliance instructions. |
| Allowed claims | An eat-first area is an organizational cue; readers may use, freeze where appropriate, retain, seek guidance, discard, ask for help, or take no action based on current authoritative information. |
| Prohibited claims | Reduced waste, savings, improved safety, illness prevention, shelf-life extension, convenience, time/stress reduction, habit success, sustainability, or universal fit. |
| Safety/accessibility | Eat-first does not mean safe/fresh; no taste-testing; do not rely on smell, appearance, or dates alone; narrow raw-food and temperature claims; account for reach, grip, vision, fatigue, illness, freezer access, children, pets, shared ownership, and incomplete sessions. |
| Outline | What eat-first is/is not; inspect what is present; choose one bounded visible area; separate organization from safety; choose a next decision; shared kitchens/capacity; stop before overhaul; sources and limits. |
| Internal links | Pantry organization; Article 05 for retained package information; food-scrap method for later disposal; dishwashing guide for cleanup constraints. |
| Visual | Illustrative open small-apartment refrigerator with ordinary foods and one grouped shelf area; no spoiled food, brands, readable dates, or safety implication. |
| Alt text | Illustrative small apartment refrigerator with a small group of containers arranged together on one shelf. |
| Channel | Pinterest: bounded eat-first check. Instagram: notice → group one area → consult guidance. No external publication. |
| Stop conditions | Pause for universal storage durations, restaurant standards, taste-testing, or drift into nutrition, savings, waste, or safety promises. |
| Status | Approved for autonomous publication implementation by owner; article must still pass repository, CI, accessibility, and production gates. |
